About Arcana

Arcana Engine is a graphics engine built from scratch with OpenGL and C++. The main purpose of this project is to provide a comprehensive, adaptable framework for creating three-dimensional graphics applications.

Arcana Engine focuses on realistic rendering and procedural content generation. One of the primary goals is to provide a platform for rendering procedurally generated terrains at planetary scales, possibly allowing users to generate and render entire solar systems.

Arcana Engine implements many features of a proper game engine; however, it isn't limited solely to building games!

About Me

My name is Nick Jordan and I am currently the sole developer of Arcana Engine. I am currently a Massachusetts Institute of Technology undergraduate studying computer science and planetary science.

I have been experimenting with OpenGL for about four years now; however, I have yet to take a formal computer graphics course (although there are a few here within the CS department that are offered).

Arcana Engine started as a game design capstone project my senior year of high school. Given that we only had four months to complete the project, it was very messy and some parts didn't work (its hard to create a game engine in four months!). I decided that I would start from scratch and focus on modularization, more complete features, and documentation.
